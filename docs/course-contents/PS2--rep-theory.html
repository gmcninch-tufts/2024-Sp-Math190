ProblemSet 2 -- Representations and characters

Posted on 2024-01-29 by George McNinch

In these exercises, all vector spaces are assumed to be finite dimensional over the field \(F = \mathbb{C}\).

  1. In this problem, we identify the character \(\chi_\Omega\) of the permutation representation \((\rho,F[\Omega])\) of a group \(G\).

    1. Let \(V\) be a vector space and let \(\Phi:V \to V\) a linear mapping If \(\mathcal{B}\) is a basis for \(V\), recall that the trace of \(\Phi\) is defined by \[\operatorname{tr}(\Phi) = \operatorname{tr}([\Phi]_\mathcal{B}).\]

      Explain why this definition is independent of the choice of basis \(\mathcal{B}\) of \(V\).

    2. Recall that the dual of \(V\) is the vector space \(V^\vee = \operatorname{Hom}_F(V,F)\) of linear functionals on \(V\).

      If \(b_1,\dots,b_n\) is a basis for \(V\), let \({b_j}^\vee:V \to F\) be defined by \({b_j}^\vee(b_i) = \delta_{i,j}\), so that \({b_j}^\vee \in V^\vee\). Show that \({b_1}^\vee,\dots,{b_n}^\vee\) is a basis for \(V^\vee\); it is known as the dual basis to \(b_1,\dots,b_n\).

    3. Prove that the trace of the linear mapping \(\Phi:V \to V\) is given by the expression \[\operatorname{tr}(\Phi) = \sum_{i=1}^n {b_i}^\vee(\Phi(b_i))\] for any choice of basis \(\mathcal{B}=\{b_1,b_2,\cdots,b_n\}\).

    4. Suppose that the finite group \(G\) acts on the finite set \(\Omega\), and consider the corresponding permutation representation \((\rho,F[\Omega])\) of \(G\). Recall that \(F[\Omega]\) is the vector space of all \(F\)-values functions on \(\Omega\), and that for \(f \in F[\Omega]\) and \(g \in G\), we have \[\rho(g)f(\omega) = f(g^{-1}\omega).\] In particular, we saw in the lecture that \[\rho(g)\delta_\omega = \delta_{g\omega}\] where \(\delta_\omega\) denotes the Dirac function at \(\omega \in \Omega\).

      Show that \[\operatorname{tr}(\rho(g)) = \#\{\omega \in \Omega \mid g\omega = \omega\}.\] Thus the character \(\chi_\Omega\) of the representation \((\rho,F[\Omega])\) is given by \[\chi_\Omega(g) = \text{the number of fixed points of the action of $g$ on $\Omega$.}\]

  2. Let \(G\) be a finite group and let \(V\) be a \(G\) representation.

    1. Let \(L\) be an irreducible representation of \(G\), and write \(\mathscr{S}\) for the set of all subrepresentations \(W\) of \(V\) for which there is an isomorphism \(W \simeq L\) of \(G\)-representations.

      Write \[V_{(L)} = \sum_{W \in \mathscr{S}} W.\] Prove that the \(V_{(L)}\) is a subrepresentation of \(V\), and that the quotient \(G\)-representation \(V/V_{(L)}\) has no subrepresentations isomorphic to \(L\).

    2. Write \(L_1,L_2,\cdots,L_r\) for the collection of all irreducible representations of \(G\).

      Prove that \(V\) is isomorphic to the direct sum \[V_{(L_1)} \oplus V_{(L_2)} \oplus \cdots \oplus V_{(L_r)}.\]

    Remark: The subrepresentation \(V_{(L)}\) is known as the isotypic component of \(V\) of type \(L\).

  3. Let \(N >0\) be a natural number, and let \(\zeta \in F^\times\) be a primitive \(N\)-th root of unity (if you like, you can take \(\zeta = e^{2\pi i/N}\)).
